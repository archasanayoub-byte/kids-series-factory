## Logline
Every night at 3:17 AM, every clock in the city stops for exactly one second — and
something looks back. Ethan Voss, a night-shift radio engineer with a scar he can't
explain and a watch permanently stuck at 3:17, is the first to notice the pattern is
getting worse. As he pulls at the thread, he finds two others who've been marked the
same way — Maya and Lena — and a silent fourth, Noah, who hears things on dead
frequencies no one else can. What they uncover is not a ghost story. It's a
surveillance program that was never supposed to be found: Project Echo.

## Genre & Tone
Mystery / psychological suspense. Grounded, quiet dread rather than jump-scare horror.
Slow-burn conspiracy pacing — Tom & Jerry-silent-cartoon energy this is NOT; this
series is dialogue-driven, adult/teen-skewing, atmospheric.

## Format
24 episodes, Season 1. Each episode: a full scene-by-scene script, image/video
generation prompts per scene, audio/music/editing notes, YouTube title + description +
tags, a thumbnail concept, 2-3 Shorts cut-downs, and a continuity-log update.

## The Rule (central mystery mechanic)
At exactly 3:17 AM, every clock in range stops for one second. Anyone awake and aware
during that second who looks toward a reflective surface (mirror, window, still water)
risks being "noticed." Being noticed means losing time — minutes, sometimes hours —
with no memory of what happened, and a new, unexplainable mark on the body afterward.

## Season 1 Structure (3 Acts)
- Act 1 — "Alone" (Episodes 1-8): Ethan discovers the rule and starts documenting it.
- Act 2 — "Echo" (Episodes 9-16): Lena and Noah join him; Project Echo is named.
- Act 3 — "The Facility" (Episodes 17-24): the trio infiltrates the source of the
  signal; season finale.

## Finale Beat (locked)
The season ends on Dr. Adrian Vail suddenly appearing in the facility's exit doorway —
confirming Project Echo has a living architect, not just old records. Sets up Season 2.
